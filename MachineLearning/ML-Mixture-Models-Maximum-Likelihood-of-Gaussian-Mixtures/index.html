Maximum Likelihood of Gaussian Mixtures

Posted on 2020-03-05 Edited on 2020-11-08 In Artificial Intelligence , Machine Learning Disqus:

Keywords: maximum likelihood, Gaussian mixtures

Maximum Likelihood[1]

Gaussian mixtures had been discussed in ‘Mixtures of Gaussians’. And once we have training data and a certain hypothesis, what we should do next is estimating the parameters of the model. Both kinds of parameters from a mixture of Gaussians

and latent variables:

we defined, need to estimate. However, when we investigated the linear model ‘Maximum Likelihood Estimation’, we considered the prediction yyy of the linear model has a Gaussian distribution, and then maximum likelihood was used to derive parameters in the model. So, we could employ the maximum likelihood again in the Gaussian mixture task, and find whether it could work well.

Maximum Likelihood Could Fail

The other differences from the single Gaussian model are the singularity of the covariance matrix and the condition xn=μj\boldsymbol{x}_n=\boldsymbol{\mu}_jxn​=μj​.

Covariance Matrix Σ\SigmaΣ is not Singular

For in the Gaussian distribution, the covariance matrix must be able to be inverted. So in our following discussion, we assume all the covariance matrice are invisible. For simplicity we take Σk=δk2I\Sigma_k=\delta_k^2 IΣk​=δk2​I where III is identity matrix.

When xn=μj\boldsymbol{x}_n=\boldsymbol{\mu}_jxn​=μj​

When a point in sample accidentally equals to the mean μj\mu_jμj​, the Gaussin distribution of the random variable xn\boldsymbol{x}_nxn​ is:

N(xn∣μk,δj2I)=12π121δj(7)\mathcal{N}(\boldsymbol{x}_n|\boldsymbol{\mu}_k,\delta_j^2I)=\frac{1}{2\pi^{\frac{1}{2}}}\frac{1}{\delta_j}\tag{7} N(xn​∣μk​,δj2​I)=2π21​1​δj​1​(7)

When the variance δj→0\delta_j\to 0δj​→0, this part goes to infinity and the whole algorithm failed.

This problem does not exist in a single Gaussian model, for the xn−μj=0\boldsymbol{x}_n-\boldsymbol{\mu}_j=0xn​−μj​=0 is not an exponent in its log-likelihood.

Conclusion

The maximum likelihood method is not suited for a Gaussian mixture model. Then we introduce the EM algorithm in the next post.

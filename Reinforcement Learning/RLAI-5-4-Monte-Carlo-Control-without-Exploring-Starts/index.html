(draft) Monte Carlo Control without Exploring Starts

Posted on 2020-04-24 Edited on 2021-02-07 In category Disqus:

Abstract:

Last post ‘Monte Carlo Control’, we come up with the method called MCES which is short for Monte Carlo with Exploring Start. It removes the assumption, infinity steps of the Monte Carlo estimation in GPI. Now, we try to remove the other assumption - exploring starts.

On-policy and Off-policy

The reason we start with exploration in MC estimation is to guarantee that every stat-action pair could be visited during the learning process. In some systems, only the optimal action of current state will be done basing on some greedy policy. So the decision of which action would be taken is all by the policy. It inspired us that we could replace the greedy policy with some other policies which could produce exploration.

Two approaches of policy we consider here:

  1. on-policy: evaluate and improve the policy to make decision
  2. off-policy: evaluate and improve the policy that is different from that used to generate action.

So far, most of methods we have talked about is on-policy method like MCSE that we discussed in the post ‘Monte Carlo Control’. In this post, our aim is to get ride of eploring starts, and the on-policy method without exploring starts would be discussed first because the off-policy methods contains exploration naturely during the process.

This modification of policy of on-policy method is making the policy soft, which means no more greedy:

The overall idea is still GPI:

  1. first-visit MC
  2. without assumption of exploring starts
  3. policy is improve to ϵ\epsilonϵ-greedy for future exploration.

This GPI methods of MC could work mainly relies on the property of GPI that it does not need a greedy policy but an improved policy(’…incomplete steps of policy evaluation or policy improvement also converge to the point of intersection.’ from Generalized Policy Iteration)

Incomplete step of policy improvement could still make the GPI converge to optimal, so we need to make sure that ϵ\epsilonϵ-greedy policy π′\pi'π′ is better than any other ϵ\epsilonϵ-soft policy π\piπ of a state(the ‘better’ has been defined in the post ‘Policy Improvement’)

Upper-Confidence-Bound Algorithm

In the post ‘Optimistic Initial Value’, we took a look at a trick which could help stationary reinforcement learning explore in the first kkk step where the kkk is the number of arms(actions). In the ε\varepsilonε-greedy algorithm, we explore randomly among all the actions with probability ε\varepsilonε. Although the random selection in ε\varepsilonε-greedy algorithm works well in a long run, the speed of convergence is low. What we do, now, is to speed it up.

The first inspiration is that the action whose value is not so far from the current optimal one could be the real optimal action. So we should explore these actions more frequently than others who is far from the current optimal one.

The second idea comes from the law of large number. The average of reward in ε\varepsilonε-greedy algorithm converge to the value of action when the time(steps) goes to infinity. However, if time(steps) is not infinity or even not so large, the average of reward has relatively large variance to approximate the value. Then we need more exploratin in these actions to make their average closer to their values.

So, now, we mix these two inspirations in the greedy algorithm and then we get a new greedy step:

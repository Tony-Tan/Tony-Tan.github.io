Initial Values in Stationary Enviroment

In the post ‘Tracking a Nonstationary Problem’, we have a look at the nonstationary problem and the constant step-size make the increment implementation framwork valid for nonstationary environment. In this post, we go back to stationary task, and focous on another elements in the 10-armed bandit testbed: the inital values:
$$
Q_1(a)
$$

for $a =1,2,\cdots,k$. In the original experiment, we set the initial value to 0, and to make the whole process more stable, I forced the first 5 step to explore in the post ‘Tracking a Nonstationary Problem’. Today, another strategy will be introduced to make the first several step of the process to explore but not exploit. This is necessary for stationary task to build prior knowledge at the begining of the process. However, this strategy for this requirement seems not helpful to nonstationary reinforcement learning task. This makes sense for we initial experiment only once, and in the nonstationary environment, the early steps have less effect as the time goes on.

If we set the initial expectation of reward by hand but not sample from the distribution of reward, it is bias from the view of statistics. However, this is not a problem, and sometimes it us very helpful. Bias does not means harmful, and setting the parameters manully can also help us inject some prior knowledge.

A good initial value of expectation of ‘the 10-armed Testbed’ is $5$. Because $5$ is certain to be bigger than any reward produced by the bandit. For example, to 10-armed bandit, the first action we chose the first arm $a=1$ and get a reward $R_1$ and it must less than $5$ for it is produced by the normal distribution of mean 0 variance 1. So, if we used a greedy strategy, we would select other arms but not the one we have selected. Then the process goes on, and every arm will be selected at least once in the first $k$ steps.

We initial the expectation of reward bigger than the reward range, then the greedy method explores in the first $k$ steps. This is just a little trike for stationary task. Although it may fail in nonstationary reinforcement learning task, it will be also used in some algorithms.

Experiment

we initial the expectation $Q_1(a)$ to $5$ and $\alpha=0.1$ (‘Incremental Implementation’), the 10-armed bandit testbed looks like:

In the early time, the $0.1$-greedy algorithm has a steep increase, but for a long period, greedy method with the optimistic initial method perfrom better.


the mysterious spikes in the figure above is because that step(step $k$) we selected the action greedly, at that time every action has been taken once and the value of them are calculated by the initial value($+5$ here) and their first reward in the whole process. Under this condition, the greedy action is a good estimation of optimal action. However, in the step $k+1$ one of the $k$ value $Q(a_i)$ would be updated by 2 rewards and the initial value $5$, and their average ($\frac{5+R_1+R_2}{3}$)is usually less than initial value and 1 reward($\frac{5+R_1}{2}$), even though the action $a_i$ is the optimal one.

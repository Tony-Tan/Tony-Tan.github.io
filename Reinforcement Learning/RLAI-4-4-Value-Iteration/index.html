(draft)Value Iteration

Posted on 2020-04-21 Edited on 2020-11-08 In category Disqus:

Abstract:

In the iteration of policy evaluation and policy improvement, the policy evaluation itself has another iteration, which is employed to calculate a optimal value function of current policy. And this iteration in policy evaluation give us an inspriation to a new method.

Value Iteration

The drawback of policy iteration is that each iteration involves policy evaluation and the policy evaluation need multiple sweeps through the state set. However, according our example, gridword, in the post ‘Policy Evaluation’ we find the greedy policy does not change after the $k$ steps of the iteration of policy evaluation and the iteration stoped at step $m$ and $k<<m$. Then, the policy interation $k+1,k+2,\cdots,m$ does not involve the policy. So we would like to truncate the iteration of policy evaluation without lossing convergence guarantees of policy iteration.

The easiest and extreme condition is that each policy evaluation contains only one iteration. And it’s called value iteration. The initial policy is a random policy:

$$
\pi_0\underbrace{\xrightarrow{E}v_{\pi_0}\xrightarrow{I}\pi_1}{\text{value iteration 0}}
\underbrace{\xrightarrow{E}v{\pi_1}\xrightarrow{I}\pi_2}{\text{value iteration 1}}\cdots
\underbrace{\xrightarrow{I}\pi\xrightarrow{E}v_{\pi_}}_{\text{value iteration *}}\tag{1}
$$

In each operation ‘$\xrightarrow{E}$’, there is only one iteration. Let’s go into step $k$ of value iteration:

  1. policy evaluation(policy $\pi_k$:’$\xrightarrow{E}$’)

$$
v_{\pi_k}=\mathbb{E}[R_{t_1}+\gamma v_{\pi_{k-1}}(S_{t+1})|S_t=s ,A_t=a]\tag{2}
$$

  1. policy improvement(value function $v_{\pi_k}$: ‘$\xrightarrow{I}$’)

$$
\begin{aligned}
\pi_{k+1}(s)&=\mathop{argmax}a v{\pi_k}\
&=\mathop{argmax}a \mathbb{E}[R{t_1}+\gamma v_{\pi_{k-1}}(S_{t+1})|S_t=s ,A_t=a]\tag{3}
\end{aligned}

$$

  1. if we combine step 1 and step 2 we get:

$$
\begin{aligned}
v_{\pi_k}&=\mathop{max}a v{\pi_{k-1}}\
&=\mathop{max}a \mathbb{E}[R{t_1}+\gamma v_{\pi_{k-1}}(S_{t+1})|S_t=s ,A_t=a]
\end{aligned}\tag{4}
$$

Then we update the interation (1) to:
$$
v_{\pi_0}\xrightarrow{M}v_{\pi_1}\xrightarrow{M}v_{\pi_2}\xrightarrow{M}\cdots \xrightarrow{M}v_{\pi_*}\tag{5}
$$
And it can also be written as:
$$
v_{0}\xrightarrow{M}v_{1}\xrightarrow{M}v_{2}\xrightarrow{M}\cdots \xrightarrow{M}v_{*}\tag{6}
$$

This is value iteration, and it converge to $v_*$ under the same conditions that guarantee the existance of $v_*$.

Value Iteration and Bellman Equation

Another way to understand value iteration is through Bellman optimal equation. And equation (4) are the same with Bellman Optimal equation. However, it’s, now, an update rule. It is also a combine of following backup diagram:

Value iteration method is much faster than policy iteration. And the general framework of truncated policy evaluation is:

$$
v_1\xrightarrow{\text{e}}v_2\xrightarrow{\text{e}}\cdots v_i\xrightarrow{\text{max}}v_{i+1}\xrightarrow{\text{e}}v_{i+2}\cdots v_j\xrightarrow{\text{max}}v_{j+1}\cdots
$$

where $j>i$. These interation looks like the ‘max’ operation is inserted into the evaluation iteration randomly. And this is just another description of policy iteration whose policy evaluation is truncated randomly.

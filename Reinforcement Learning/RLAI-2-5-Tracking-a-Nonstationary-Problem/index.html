Posted on 2020-04-08 Edited on 2020-11-08 In category Disqus:

Keywords: step-size, converge condition, weighted average, stationary, nonstationary, exponential recency-weighted averge

Constant Step-size

To a stationary problem we have talked about, such as 10-armed bandits in post ‘the 10-armed Testbed’, the reward of each action has a invariable distritbution, and we call this environment is stationary. The balance between exploration and exploitation had been discussed to get a relatively high reward during a long run. However, in most reinforcement learning task, the enviroment changes all the time which is called nonstationary. what we would like to investage today is to find out whether we can use the framwork, the ‘increment implementation’, to solve the nonstationary task.

To a stationary problem, we have used the average of rewards that recieved from start of experiment to approximate the value of an action:

Qn=R1+R2+⋯+Rn−1n−1(1)Q_{n} = \frac{R_1+R_2+\cdots+R_{n-1}}{n-1}\tag{1} Qn​=n−1R1​+R2​+⋯+Rn−1​​(1)

To make the compute of the value QnQ_{n}Qn​ efficiently, we transform the equation (1) and get its increment form:

Qn+1=Qn+1n(Rn−Qn)(2)Q_{n+1} = Q_{n} + \frac{1}{n}(R_n-Q_{n})\tag{2} Qn+1​=Qn​+n1​(Rn​−Qn​)(2)

and based on the equantion(2), we got the framwork refered to as ‘increment implementation’:

NewEstimate←OldEstimate+stepsize×[Target−OldEstimate](3)\text{NewEstimate}\gets\text{OldEstimate}+\text{stepsize}\times[\text{Target}-\text{OldEstimate}]\tag{3} NewEstimate←OldEstimate+stepsize×[Target−OldEstimate](3)

To average all the rewards, we employed a changing step-size α=1n\alpha = \frac{1}{n}α=n1​. And this step-size goes to 000 as the time goes to infinite. However, if we set the step-size to a constant value that α∈(0,1]\alpha \in ( 0,1 ]α∈(0,1], the ‘increment implementation’ would behavior differently:

Qn+1=Qn+α[Rn−Qn]=αRn+(1−α)Qn=αRn+(1−α)(Qn−1+α[Rn−1−Qn−1])=αRn+α(1−α)Rn−1+(1−α)2Qn−1=αRn+α(1−α)Rn−1+(1−α)2Qn−1=αRn+α(1−α)Rn−1+α(1−α)2Rn−2+⋯=(1−α)nQ1+∑i=1nα(1−α)n−iRi(4)\begin{aligned} Q_{n+1} &= Q_n +\alpha [R_n -Q_n]\\ &= \alpha R_n +(1-\alpha)Q_n\\ &= \alpha R_n +(1-\alpha)(Q_{n-1} +\alpha [R_{n-1} -Q_{n-1}])\\ &= \alpha R_n +\alpha(1-\alpha) R_{n-1} + (1-\alpha)^2Q_{n-1}\\ &= \alpha R_n +\alpha(1-\alpha) R_{n-1} + (1-\alpha)^2Q_{n-1}\\ &= \alpha R_n +\alpha(1-\alpha) R_{n-1} + \alpha(1-\alpha)^2 R_{n-2} +\cdots \\ &= (1-\alpha)^n Q_1 +\sum_{i=1}^{n}\alpha(1-\alpha)^{n-i} R_{i} \end{aligned}\tag{4} Qn+1​​=Qn​+α[Rn​−Qn​]=αRn​+(1−α)Qn​=αRn​+(1−α)(Qn−1​+α[Rn−1​−Qn−1​])=αRn​+α(1−α)Rn−1​+(1−α)2Qn−1​=αRn​+α(1−α)Rn−1​+(1−α)2Qn−1​=αRn​+α(1−α)Rn−1​+α(1−α)2Rn−2​+⋯=(1−α)nQ1​+i=1∑n​α(1−α)n−iRi​​(4)

So according to the equation (4), the behaviors of constant step-size can be concluded as:

  1. for (1−α)n+∑i=1nα(1−α)n−i=1(1-\alpha)^n+\sum_{i=1}^{n}\alpha(1-\alpha)^{n-i} =1(1−α)n+∑i=1n​α(1−α)n−i=1 and α∈(0,1]\alpha \in (0,1]α∈(0,1] then the equation (4) is a weighted average.
  2. the coefficient of each reward RiR_iRi​ is α(1−α)n−i\alpha(1-\alpha)^{n-i}α(1−α)n−i represent that when iii is big the reward has higher weighted and vice versa. And the bigger iii means the step is more recent action.
  3. α(1−α)n−i\alpha(1-\alpha)^{n-i}α(1−α)n−i is an exponential weight. And equation (4) is also called ‘exponential recency-weighted average’.
  4. if α=1\alpha=1α=1, beside the reward of step nnn, RnR_nRn​, others weight are all 000, that means the value is only decided by the last reward.

Converge Condition

So far, we have studied two choices of step-size. However, we want to know how to vary the step-size for different task. The step-size of equation (1), α=1n\alpha = \frac{1}{n}α=n1​ guarantees the final result converge to the expectation of reward, the value, in probability by the law of large number. And we list the conditions of convergence with probability 111 here:

  • Condition 1:

∑n=1∞αn(a)=∞(5)\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\alpha_n(a)=\infty\tag{5} n=1∑∞​αn​(a)=∞(5)

  • Condition 2:

∑n=1∞αn2(a)<∞(6)\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\alpha_n^2(a)<\infty\tag{6} n=1∑∞​αn2​(a)<∞(6)

The equation (5) make sure the step-sizes are large enough to overcome:

  1. the initial condition(that is set by us)
  2. random fluctuation

The equation (6) take the step decrease as the time goes.

These two conditions guarantee the converge of stationary environment reinforcement learning task. But it has a lot of drawbacks:

  1. the value calculated by the step-size under these conditions converge slowly
  2. this kind of step-size needs considerable tuning
  3. these conditions are seldem used in practical task

The constant step-size αn(a)=constant\alpha_n(a)=\text{constant}αn​(a)=constant does converge for it mismatch the second condition.
